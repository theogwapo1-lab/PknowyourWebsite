With the recent news of a double murder committed by a police officer that happened in my country, Kisapmata has never been more relevant than ever. The mystery drama is based on a true murder-suicide case that happened back in 1961, where a police man committed three murders and suicide after a tumultuous case of domestic dispute. Machismo is heavily shown in this film through the character of Vic Silayan, who used his previous occupation as a police officer and brusque attitude to terrorize his family, in guise of protection. Review by bey ★★★★★ Liked

a film’s realism can often be mischievous. the realism that makes kisapmata so disturbing is that it is a film that is not just a story told to elicit shock/suspense. the story reflects the true horror of the power wielded by the oppressors of an unjust patriarchal system. the power that is the true evil is not a figment of the imagination, for power is evil and violent even in a system that is not patriarchal.

kisapmata is a film that touches and reflects the injustice of a patriarchal society. it is not just a film; it is an emotional experience. it is the disturbing experience that the viewer must face. the experience that is, unfortunately, essential to see the true horror of the reality that can be most disturbing to the audience—one that is haunting because it reveals a cruelty that is tragic in its very reality.

Review by Edeva ★★★★★ Liked

I’m putting this on the top of the list for movies that make me feel trapped. What a beautiful piece of Filipino cinema that I wish I saw while I was at film school. I came into this thinking it would be just another Filipino drama but what I wasn’t expecting was it to lean more thriller and horror in some aspects. Mike de Leon really captured the essence of a toxic Filipino family dynamic with the pacing, cinematography, the score and direction. Also those black and white dream sequences were my favourite. Vic Silayan plays the father so well with his charming facade only to see the truth in his intentions as the story unfolds. I can’t wait to explore more Mike de Leon films. Rest in peace 🥀
